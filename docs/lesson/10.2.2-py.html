Design a Video Streaming Platform (YouTube)

Intuition

A video streaming platform is a content processing and distribution pipeline that transforms raw uploads into multi-bitrate segments served from edge locations close to viewers. Adaptive bitrate streaming is the key technique that enables smooth playback across varying network conditions.

Design a Video Streaming Platform (YouTube)

Requirements

  • Upload, transcode, and stream videos to millions of concurrent viewers
  • Support adaptive bitrate streaming for varying network conditions
  • Handle content recommendation, search, and engagement features
  • Minimize startup latency and buffering during playback

Architecture

Uploaded videos go through a transcoding pipeline that produces multiple resolutions and formats. Transcoded segments are stored on object storage and distributed via CDN. A video metadata service stores titles, descriptions, and engagement data. An adaptive streaming protocol (HLS/DASH) serves video segments based on client bandwidth.

Key Components

  • Upload Service: Accepts video uploads, validates, and queues for transcoding
  • Transcoding Pipeline: Distributed workers (FFmpeg-based) that encode videos into multiple bitrates (240p to 4K) and segment them
  • CDN: Edge servers cache popular video segments close to viewers
  • Streaming Service: Serves manifest files (m3u8/mpd) and video segments via HTTP
  • Recommendation Engine: ML-based collaborative filtering and content-based suggestions

Scaling

Use a DAG-based transcoding pipeline for parallel processing of different resolutions. Pre-warm CDN for trending videos. Use edge computing for live stream processing. Shard metadata by video ID.

Trade-offs

  • More transcoding profiles cover more devices but multiply storage and processing costs
  • Pre-loading video segments reduces buffering but wastes bandwidth if the user navigates away

Exercises

  1. Design a Transcoding Pipeline

    Build a DAG-based transcoding workflow where an uploaded video is split into segments, each segment is transcoded to multiple resolutions in parallel, and results are merged into a streaming manifest. Handle failures by retrying individual segment-resolution pairs.

    Hint

    Model the pipeline as a directed acyclic graph: split -> [transcode_240p, transcode_480p, transcode_720p, transcode_1080p] -> generate_manifest. Use a workflow engine like Temporal or Step Functions.

  2. Implement Adaptive Bitrate Selection

    Create a client-side algorithm that monitors download throughput and buffer level, then selects the optimal video quality for the next segment. Switch up gradually but drop quality immediately when bandwidth drops.

    Hint

    Track a moving average of throughput. If throughput > next_higher_bitrate * 1.5 for 3 segments, switch up. If buffer drops below 2 seconds, immediately switch to the lowest available bitrate.

Common Problems & Solutions

1 A viral video causes CDN cache misses and overwhelms the origin storage with millions of concurrent requests.
Cause: The video was not pre-cached at edge locations, and the sudden traffic spike causes a thundering herd at the origin.
Solution: Implement request coalescing at the CDN (only one origin fetch per cache miss). Pre-warm CDN for videos with rapidly increasing view counts. Use a predictive model to identify potentially viral content and proactively cache it.
2 The transcoding pipeline creates a huge backlog during peak upload hours, delaying video availability by hours.
Cause: Fixed-capacity transcoding workers cannot handle upload spikes, and all resolutions are transcoded before any version is available.
Solution: Transcode the most common resolution (720p) first and make the video available immediately. Process remaining resolutions in the background. Use auto-scaling worker pools that expand during peak hours.
